Is it actually accurate?

Tested against privacy policies that were scored by hand as a baseline.

  • Accuracy vs. human-scored policies70.3%
  • Improvement over the previous model+10%
  • Previous model's accuracy (before this version)60%

The previous version used a classical machine learning model (LightGBM), which struggled with nuance and context in longer policies. Swapping in an LLM for scoring was what closed most of that gap — and accuracy should keep improving as the underlying model does.

Questions people ask

Does it read the whole policy, or just skim it?

It reads the full text of the policy pulled from the page, then scores it against a set of GDPR-based questions — it's not just keyword matching.

Can I trust the score completely?

Treat it as a strong starting point, not a legal verdict. It's currently about 70% aligned with how a human reviewer would score the same policy, and that accuracy is expected to keep improving.

What if I only care about certain things, like location tracking?

That's what the category sliders are for. Weight the categories that matter to you, and the overall score reflects your priorities instead of a one-size-fits-all number.

What's the "readability" number for?

It's a separate reading-level score (based on two standard readability formulas) that tells you how hard the policy itself is to understand — a policy can score well on privacy and still be a slog to read.
